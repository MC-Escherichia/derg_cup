The NH Problem

Let's get the uncomfortable truth out of the way: NH owns this pool right now, and the model agrees emphatically.

The Monte Carlo simulation — 20,000 runs of the remaining tournament, sampling team strength from evolved post-group-stage Elo ratings — gives NH a 55.3% probability of winning the pool outright. In a 16-person pool, that number is almost absurd. Second place (PB2) sits at 14.4%. The field combined gets 44.7%.

How did this happen? Two words: Argentina and Norway.

Argentina was dominant — 9 points, 8 goals scored, 1 conceded, tied for the group stage's highest total. They beat Algeria 3-0, Austria 2-0, and Jordan 3-1 without breaking a sweat. Their evolved Elo after these results makes them the tournament's clear favorite by a significant margin, projecting to an average of 31.9 pool points by tournament end if they win it all.

Norway wasn't far behind. A 4-1 win over Iraq in round one set the tone. They stumbled 1-4 to France in the final group match but had already clinched second — a calculated cruise to the knockouts with 6 points.

Even Scotland — eliminated in Group C as expected — contributed a solid 3 points (a win over Haiti before losses to Morocco and Brazil). NH's floor going into the group stage was probably 15 points. They scored 18.

The other 15 players in this pool want the same thing: Argentina to lose. The bracket, unfortunately, does not make it easy. Argentina opens with Cape Verde.

The Uncomfortable Math

For anyone other than NH to win this pool, Argentina almost certainly needs to lose — likely before the quarterfinals. The model's projected final score for NH averages 31.9 points. The next-highest averages are PB2 (25.6) and NC (25.5), meaning even in their best-case scenarios, those players need NH to run cold.

The must-watch first-round match: Ivory Coast vs. Norway (M78). Ivory Coast is JF's last team. Norway is NH's second horse. JF desperately needs the win; NH desperately needs it not to happen. The model gives Norway the edge, but Ivory Coast is a legitimate top-30 Elo team. It won't be a walkover.

Twenty thousand simulated tournaments say the same thing: NH is the heavy favorite, the gap is real, and the only cure is an Argentine defeat.
